Introduction

This guide demonstrates how to use the settings that control the scale of a timeline when it is drawn on a PDF. Each timeline has two settings for its scale. Scale unit controls the amount of time between each unit on the scale, because each type of timeline uses different time units this setting varies between the different timeline types. The number of scale units in a timeline is calculated automatically using the scale unit, the smallest and greatest last time unit in the timeline's events. Scale unit length controls the distance in CM between each scale unit.

Example

This age timeline has...

  • A scale unit of 5 years.
  • A scale unit length of 4 cm.
  • A first event at 6 years.
  • A last event at 22 Years.

The resulting pdf timeline will have the following scale...

  • Starts at 5 years (6 years from first event rounded down to the nearest scale unit of 5 years).
  • Ends at 25 years (22 years from last event rounded up to the nearest scale unit of 5 years).
  • Has units at 10 years, 15 years and 20 years (every 5 years between start and end).
  • Is 16 cm long (25 years - 5 years = 20 years, 20 years / 5 years per scale unit = 4 scale units, 4 scale units * scale unit length of 4 cm = 16 cm).
